KS5 Student Outcomes

This project involved building a predictive model to identify Key Stage 5 students at risk of underperforming, allowing for timely interventions. We utilized a combination of PyTorch for deep learning aspects and Scikit-learn for traditional machine learning models. Key features included student demographics, attendance records, prior attainment data, and engagement metrics.

The methodology involved extensive exploratory data analysis (EDA) to understand student performance trends and identify influential factors. Various models were experimented with, including logistic regression, random forests, and a simple neural network. Model performance was evaluated using metrics such as precision, recall, F1-score, and AUC, with a focus on maximizing recall to ensure no at-risk student was missed.

The insights gained from this project were used to inform academic support programs and resource allocation, ultimately aiming to improve overall student outcomes. The system provides educators with an actionable dashboard to monitor student progress and flag those requiring immediate attention.

Hourly Energy Forecasting

XGBoost and time‑series EDA to predict energy usage.

XGBoost Pandas Analytics

Hourly Energy Forecasting

This project focused on developing a robust model for hourly energy consumption forecasting, crucial for optimizing energy management and reducing costs. The primary tool used was XGBoost, a powerful gradient boosting framework, due to its efficiency and performance on structured data.

The process began with extensive time-series exploratory data analysis (EDA) using Pandas. This involved identifying trends, seasonality, and anomalies in historical energy usage data, along with incorporating external factors like temperature and day of the week. Feature engineering played a significant role, where lagged features, rolling means, and Fourier terms were created to capture temporal dependencies.

The model was trained and validated using a time-series cross-validation approach to ensure its robustness to future data. Performance was measured using metrics such as Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE). The resulting forecasts provide valuable insights for energy providers and consumers to make informed decisions about energy production and consumption.
